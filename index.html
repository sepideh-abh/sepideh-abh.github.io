About me

Hi! I am Sepideh, a 25-year-old Physics researcher from Tehran, Iran. I finished my bachelor of science in physics at the Sharif University of Technology.
The most significant part of physics for me is that you can find it anywhere, biology, chemistry, and all engineering fields. It's almost impossible to take even a small step toward solving a problem in any of the mentioned fields without using physics.
As a physicist who is passionate about astronomy and astrophysics, I am fascinated by how these fields rely on all of physics—including quantum mechanics, general relativity, and thermodynamics—to describe the universe's most extraordinary phenomena, such as white dwarfs, neutron stars, and black holes. Despite our progress, there are still many gaps in our understanding, and the rapid flow of new data from cutting-edge telescopes continues to challenge and refine our knowledge. I'm excited to be part of this time of discovery, where unexpected findings force us to rethink what we thought we knew and explore new ideas. The chance to think about such deep questions makes me a happy researcher in this fascinating field.
